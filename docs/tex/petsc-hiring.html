The PETSc team is now hiring.

We are looking for people with all levels of experience who are interested in the development and support of large-scale numerical software tools using practical, cutting-edge techniques. Candidates should have a strong background in
  • C/C++ programming,
  • computing in high-performance parallel and distributed environments, and
  • software development on UNIX systems.
Additional desirable skills include:
  • strong background in numerical analysis and the numerical solution of PDEs,
  • experience with large-scale software projects,
  • Tcl/Tk programming,
  • MPI,
  • JAVA, and
  • software development on Windows NT systems.
Types of positions available:
  • Undergraduate students - Argonne participates in the DOE's Science and Engineering Research Semester (SERS) program for upper-level undergraduate students. There are also co-op appointments

  • Graduate students - Argonne offers graduate students a variety of temporary jobs, which are usually for a summer or semester-in-residence. In addition, several DOE graduate fellowship programs exist. For further information, see
    • Givens Research Associates
    • DOE Computational Science Graduate Fellowship Program
    • Lab Grad Positions

  • Visiting Faculty Positions - Summer program or semester program.

  • Software developers - These positions are for individuals with a Masters or Ph.D. degree in computer science or a related discipline. For further information.

  • Post-docs - These positions are for individuals who have received a Ph.D. in computer science or a related discipline not more than three years prior to the beginning of the appointment, which can range from one to three years in duration. These positions are joint research/development positions. Depending on your previous research focus, you will select projects that coordinate well with current activities in the MCS Division.

    In addition, the DOE Distinguished Postdoctoral Research Program provides an opportunity for individuals to propose a research plan in coordination with a DOE lab.

  • Research scientists - These positions would be either term or permanent positions in the MCS Division, devoted to research and development of numerical software tools and related infrastructure.


Specific projects these positions may involve include

  • development of interactive user interfaces with JAVA or Tcl/Tk,
  • development of PDE discretization libraries,
  • development of parallel PDE solution visualization tools,
  • implementation of parallel grid management libraries,
  • implementation of parallel preconditioners,
  • development of research application codes that use PETSc.
These positions also have the possibility of extensive collaboration with other software development projects in the MCS Division of Argonne.

The Mathematics and Computer Science Division supports a virtual reality laboratory, and an IBM Scalable POWERparallel (SP) system. This innovative machine is based on IBM's powerful RISC/6000 technology and features 128 processors, each with 128 megabytes of memory.

For more information concerning the Mathematics and Computer Science Division and its facilities, see http://www.mcs.anl.gov. Argonne is located in the southwestern Chicago suburbs, offering the advantages of affordable housing, good schools, and easy access to the cultural attractions of the city.

You may connect Jan Griffin, griffin@mcs.anl.gov, with specific questions regarding these positions.

Argonne is an affirmative action/equal opportunity employer.
