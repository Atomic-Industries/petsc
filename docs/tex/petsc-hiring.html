The PETSc team is now hiring.

We are looking for people with all levels of experience who are interested in the development and support of large-scale numerical software tools using practical, cutting-edge techniques. Candidates should have a strong background in
  • C/C++ programming,
  • computing in high-performance parallel and distributed environments
  • software development on UNIX systems.
Additional desirable skills include:
  • strong background in numerical analysis and the numerical solution of PDEs,
  • experience with large-scale software projects,
  • Tcl/Tk programming,
  • MPI,
  • JAVA,
  • software development on Windows NT systems.
Types of positions available:
  • Undergraduate students - Argonne participates in the DOE's Science and Engineering Research Semester (SERS) program for upper-level undergraduate students.

  • Graduate students - Argonne offers a variety of temporary jobs for graduate students. These are usually for a summer or semester-in-residence. For further information.

  • Software developers - Positions for those with a Masters or Ph. D. Degree in computer science or related disciplines. For further information.

  • Post-docs - These positions are for individuals who have received a Ph.D. in Computer Science or a related discipline not more than three years prior to the beginning of the appointment, which can be from one to three years in duration. These positions are joint research/development positions. Depending on your previous research focus, you will select projects that coordinate well with current activities in the MCS Division.

  • Research scientists - These would be either term or permanent positions in the MCS Division devoted to research and development of numerical software tools and related infrastructure.


The Mathematics and Computer Science Division supports an advanced computational environment that includes high-performance scientific workstations, a scientific visualization and virtual reality laboratory, and state-of-the-art parallel computers. The centerpiece of Argonne's current parallel computing environment is an IBM Scalable POWERparallel (SP) system. This innovative machine is based on IBM's powerful RISC/6000 technology and features 128 processors, each with 128 megabytes of memory.

For more information concerning the Mathematics and Computer Science Division and its facilities, see http://www.mcs.anl.gov. Argonne is located in the southwestern Chicago suburbs, offering the advantages of affordable housing, good schools, and easy access to the cultural attractions of the city.

You may connect Jan Griffin, griffin@mcs.anl.gov, with specific questions regarding these positions.

Argonne is an affirmative action/equal opportunity employer.
